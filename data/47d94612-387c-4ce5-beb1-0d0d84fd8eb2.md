On July 31st, 2018, a security researcher identified that a GitHub personal access token with recently elevated scopes had been leaked from Homebrew's Jenkins instance. This token provided `git push` access to the `Homebrew/brew` and `Homebrew/homebrew-core` repositories.

Within a few hours of the report, the compromised credentials were revoked, replaced, and sanitized within Jenkins to prevent future exposure. GitHub Support confirmed that the token had not been used to perform any unauthorized pushes to the affected repositories during the period of elevated scopes.

As a result, no packages were compromised, and no user action was required due to this incident. The Homebrew team emphasized that the project's security was not breached in a way that affected users or packages.

Remediation steps included updating `Homebrew/brew` and `Homebrew/homebrew-core` to prevent non-administrators from pushing directly to `master`. Most other repositories in the Homebrew organization were also updated to require CI checks from a pull request to pass before changes could be pushed to `master`. Maintainers were also requested to review and prune their personal access tokens and disable SMS fallback for 2FA.
