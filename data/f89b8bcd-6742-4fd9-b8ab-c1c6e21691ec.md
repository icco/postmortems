Google announced the winding down of its Currents service, with a full deprecation scheduled for July 5, 2023. This strategic decision aims to consolidate community experiences within Google Workspace, focusing efforts on Google Chat.

The transition involves migrating existing Currents data to spaces in Google Chat. Workspace administrators were provided guidance and the option for automated data migration. Administrators have until August 8, 2023, to export any remaining Currents data using Google Takeout before it becomes permanently unavailable.

The primary reason for this change is to move away from a separate, siloed community platform and integrate these functionalities directly into Google Chat, offering a more modern and enterprise-grade experience. This allows for better integration with other Google Workspace services.

Customer impact involves all Google Currents users and administrators needing to adapt to Google Chat spaces for community engagement. Administrators are responsible for ensuring data is exported if needed.

As a remediation and replacement, Google is significantly investing in and enhancing Google Chat with numerous new community features. These include announcements, Google Groups integration for membership management, post-level metrics, advanced moderation tools, data loss prevention (DLP), and expanded third-party app integrations, aiming to provide a robust platform for hybrid teams.
