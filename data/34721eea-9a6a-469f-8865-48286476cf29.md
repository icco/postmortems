On June 21, 2022, Cloudflare experienced an outage affecting traffic in 19 of its data centers. The incident began at 06:27 UTC when a network configuration change was deployed to Multi-Colo PoP (MCP) enabled locations. By 06:58 UTC, the first data center was brought back online, and all affected data centers were operational by 07:42 UTC, with the incident formally closed at 09:00 UTC.

The outage was triggered by a change to BGP prefix advertisement policies. A re-ordering of terms in the policy statement inadvertently caused a critical subset of site-local prefixes to be withdrawn. This withdrawal prevented Cloudflare engineers from easily reaching affected locations and also caused the internal load balancing system, Multimog, to stop functioning within the MCPs.

The root cause was identified as an incorrect re-ordering of terms within a BGP policy statement during a rollout aimed at standardizing BGP communities. Specifically, the `REJECT-THE-REST` term was moved before the `ADV-SITE-LOCALS` terms, leading to the immediate rejection and withdrawal of site-local prefixes.

Customer impact was significant, as the 19 affected data centers, despite representing only 4% of Cloudflare's total network, handled 50% of global traffic. Users relying on Cloudflare services through these locations experienced an inability to access websites and services.

Remediation involved reverting the problematic change. Cloudflare has outlined several immediate follow-up steps, including improving change procedures and automation for MCP-specific deployments, redesigning the problematic policy statement to prevent similar re-ordering issues, and enhancing automation for staggered rollouts and automated commit-confirm rollbacks to reduce future impact and resolution times.
