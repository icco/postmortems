On December 15th, 2021, at 00:17 UTC, PagerDuty initiated a DNS configuration change within its infrastructure. This deployment immediately affected the company's container orchestration cluster, leading to widespread service disruption.

The root cause was identified as a defect within the new DNS configuration. This flaw was not detected during testing in pre-production environments, allowing the faulty configuration to be deployed to the production system.

The defect resulted in all services running within the container orchestration cluster being unable to resolve DNS queries. This critical failure likely caused significant unavailability or degraded performance across various PagerDuty services, impacting customers.

While specific remediation details are not provided, the incident would have necessitated a rollback of the faulty configuration and a review of deployment and testing procedures to prevent similar occurrences in the future.
