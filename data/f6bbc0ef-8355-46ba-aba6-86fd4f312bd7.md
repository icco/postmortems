---

A recent change to Heroku's core application, responsible for managing the underlying infrastructure of the Common Runtime, introduced a dependency upgrade. This upgrade led to a timing lock issue, significantly reducing the throughput of task workers. Concurrently, the system failed to scale up appropriately to handle an increased workload, causing the application's work queue to build up.

A critical contributing factor was the incorrect configuration of alerts, which prevented immediate notification when new router instances were not initializing correctly on startup. These router instances, despite serving live traffic, were incorrectly flagged as being in the wrong boot state. Consequently, normal processes deleted these instances due to failing readiness checks.

The deletion of active router instances caused a degradation of the associated runtime cluster, as the autoscaling group struggled to create new instances quickly enough. This resulted in a reduced pool of available router instances, leading to requests failing as the incoming traffic volume exceeded the capacity of the limited router pool. Customers began to experience issues with the Heroku service at this point.

While specific remediation steps are not detailed in the provided summary, the resolution would have involved addressing the timing lock issue, rectifying the scaling failures, and correcting the misconfigured alerts that led to the deletion of active router instances. The system eventually stabilized as new router instances were brought online and the underlying issues were resolved.
