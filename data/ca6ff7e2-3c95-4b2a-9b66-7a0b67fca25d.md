On Saturday, October 17, 2020, at approximately 10:45 PM CET, Keepthescore.co experienced a critical incident when its production database was accidentally deleted. The website was taken offline for 30 minutes for restoration efforts.

The incident occurred when a developer executed a function intended to delete and recreate a local development database. Despite the function's code explicitly setting the host to 'localhost', it inadvertently connected to and wiped the live production database. The exact mechanism that allowed this misconnection is still under investigation.

This accidental deletion resulted in the loss of over 300,000 scoreboards and their associated data. Due to the daily backup schedule provided by DigitalOcean, any data created or updated between 3:47 PM CET and 11:21 PM CET on October 17, 2020, was permanently lost, as the restored backup predated this window.

Keepthescore.co utilized DigitalOcean's managed database service, which facilitated a quick recovery from a daily backup. The service was restored by 11:15 PM CET, approximately 30 minutes after the incident began. The team plans to investigate the root cause of the production connection and implement measures to prevent a recurrence of this specific error.
